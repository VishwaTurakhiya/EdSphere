University Overview

Harvard University, founded in 1636, is one of the oldest and most prestigious universities in the world. It is located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, and is renowned for its academic excellence, research output, and distinguished faculty. The university offers a wide range of undergraduate, graduate, and professional programs across various fields.

Harvard's mission is to educate leaders who make a difference globally through the pursuit of knowledge, critical thinking, and leadership. The university's vision is to create a better future for humanity through research, education, and innovation.

Academic Programs

Undergraduate Programs

Harvard College offers a world-class liberal arts education, providing students with the flexibility to choose from a diverse range of disciplines.

Graduate Programs

Harvard University offers graduate programs across various fields such as law, business, medicine, public health, and education. Its graduate schools include:

  • Harvard Law School
  • Harvard Business School
  • Harvard Medical School
  • Harvard Kennedy School
  • Harvard Graduate School of Education

Online Learning

Harvard offers online learning programs through Harvard Online, making education accessible to a global audience in areas such as computer science, data science, and business management.

Admissions Information

Undergraduate Admissions

Harvard is known for its highly selective admissions process, evaluating candidates based on academic excellence, extracurricular activities, essays, and interviews.

The application deadlines are typically early action (November 1) and regular decision (January 1). Harvard accepts SAT/ACT scores and evaluates applicants holistically.

Graduate Admissions

Each Harvard graduate school has different admission requirements, but they generally include academic transcripts, standardized test scores, letters of recommendation, and a personal statement.

Campus Life

Harvard University offers a vibrant campus life, with over 450 student organizations, including academic, cultural, political, and recreational groups. Students can engage in various activities ranging from arts festivals to sports competitions.

Clubs and Societies

Students can participate in various clubs like the Harvard Crimson newspaper, Harvard Model United Nations, and more.

Sports and Recreation

Harvard has a wide range of sports facilities and offers many varsity and intramural sports, including football, basketball, and rowing.

Research and Innovation

Harvard is at the forefront of research, with groundbreaking work being done across a variety of disciplines. The university houses several research centers, such as the Wyss Institute for Biologically Inspired Engineering and the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics.

Harvard is also known for fostering innovation through its Harvard Innovation Labs, which help students and alumni start and grow businesses.
